Other Works by Lewis Caroll

First Edition Cover of Alice in Wonderland

"Alice's Adventures in Wonderland," the first edition of Lewis Carroll's classic tale, introduces readers to a curious young girl named Alice who falls into a whimsical, nonsensical world filled with peculiar characters and absurd situations.

Through the Looking-Glass

"Through the Looking-Glass," the sequel to Lewis Carroll's "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland," follows Alice as she steps through a mirror into a mirror-image world, where everything is reversed and logic is even more topsy-turvy.

The Hunting of the Stark

Published in 1876, it is a nonsense poem that tells the story of a group of characters on a quest to hunt the mysterious and elusive creature known as the Snark. The poem is known for its playful language, absurdity, and clever wordplay and is often considered a classic of Victorian literature.
